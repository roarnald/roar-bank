# Context

The Frontend team had no structured mechanism for sharing technical knowledge. Individual developers accumulated expertise in silos. Ronald had seen the value of code reviews for learning, and wanted to extend that to broader technical exposure.

# Problem

Developers were not getting consistent exposure to new technologies, architectural patterns, or cross-repository learnings. Senior knowledge was not being systematically distributed.

# Actions

**2022 — Re-initiated Frontend Sharing:**
- Restarted the program with 13+ sessions hosted in one year.
- Personally hosted 3 sessions: Code Reusability, Recursive Rendering, Next JS Shell & Terminal, Unit Testing.
- Coordinated and lined up speakers from outside Frontend (Design, Backend, App, DevOps, PM).
- Archived session notes on Notion for asynchronous access.
- Programme attracted interest from Blockchain team, who requested to sit in.

**2023 — Company-Wide Expansion:**
- Took up the Frontend Company Architecture Sharing slot — public speaking to the entire company.
- Coached and trained developers to present to a company audience. Ran practice sessions, gave feedback on delivery. Observed significant growth in presenters.

**2024 — Leadership Delegation:**
- Delegated organisation of Frontend Sharing to senior developers as IC roles. Allowed seniors to gain public speaking and event planning experience.

**2025 — Cross-Company:**
- Jazz and Jane led the program; invited external company Aquarix to participate as speaker.
- Ronald was key point of contact for cross-company coordination with Aquarix.

# Impact

- Program ran continuously across 4+ years.
- Multiple junior and mid-level developers grew into confident public speakers.
- Blockchain team participation indicated cross-team cultural reach.
- Joanne (2022): *"You have shown outstanding performance as a Lead, and done well in maintaining a strong learning culture and knowledge sharing within the team."*
- Became a flagship example of Ronald's "leader of leaders" philosophy.
