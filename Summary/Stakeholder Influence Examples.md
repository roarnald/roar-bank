# Stakeholder Influence Examples

*All examples sourced directly from appraisals and solution proposals.*

## Category 1: Overriding or Redirecting a Decision Upward

### 1.1 Overrode PM Direction — Coin Backoffice Rewrite (2021)
**Stakeholder:** Product Manager
**Context:** PM directed a minimal fork of the legacy CASH repository for speed.
**Influence:** Assessed the long-term risk, made the case directly, and proceeded with a full rewrite — overriding the initial directive.
**Outcome:** Coin Backoffice became the benchmark quality codebase. PM direction was not revisited.
**Evidence:** 2021 Appraisal (Productivity & Quality, rated 5/5); co-appraiser noted "outstanding leadership skills."
**Wikilink:** [[2021-01-coin-backoffice-rewrite]]

---

### 1.2 Proposed and Implemented SA Review Gate (2024)
**Stakeholder:** PM team, Frontend Leads
**Context:** New projects were being set up by copying old codebases with no architecture review, accumulating debt from day one.
**Influence:** Proposed an Architecture Review gate in the 2023 appraisal; implemented it in 2024 as Software Architect. Applied to Rollball, PNL, and credit draw results.
**Outcome:** New projects started with better foundations; developers exposed to architecture thinking earlier.
**Evidence:** 2023 Appraisal (Suggestion/Feedback); 2024 Appraisal (New workflow section).
**Wikilink:** [[2024-01-sa-review-process]]

---

### 1.3 Advocated Against "Ship Flawed Code Earlier" Norm (2023)
**Stakeholder:** PM Team, Frontend Leadership
**Context:** A prevalent tendency was to copy legacy codebases for new projects to hit deadlines, even when better tools existed.
**Influence:** Consistently argued for taking the time to research modern tooling; introduced Svelte, Astro JS, Next.js, MDX across successive projects despite pressure to copy-paste the familiar.
**Outcome:** Team adopted multiple new frameworks and patterns; developer productivity improved measurably (MDX: 90% productivity improvement on content pages).
**Evidence:** 2023 Appraisal — Time Management section: *"I don't want to just copy a knowingly flawed and legacy codebase blindly for a new project, just to barely meet the deadline."*

## Category 2: Influencing Peers (Lateral, No Authority)

### 2.1 Proposed Urgency Tagging — Peer Process Change (2021)
**Stakeholder:** Senior developers, team leads
**Context:** Code review tension between reviewers (coaching mode) and developers with urgent fixes.
**Influence:** Investigated root cause through individual developer interviews, then brought a concrete proposal to the leads. Had no authority to implement — needed buy-in from Tsai Yu and Joanne.
**Outcome:** Urgency Tagging adopted; positive feedback at 2-month follow-up.
**Evidence:** 2021 Appraisal (Learning Ability section).
**Wikilink:** [[2021-11-urgency-tagging-workflow]]

---

### 2.2 UIUX Team Dissolution — Cross-Team Structural Change (2023)
**Stakeholder:** UIUX Team developers, UIUX Lead, Frontend Internal Management
**Context:** Ronald had no authority over the UIUX team. Dissolving it required management buy-in and individual consent from every affected developer.
**Influence:** Built a data-backed business case about the skills market mismatch; committed to a personal guarantee (custom team structure for any dissenter); interviewed every UIUX developer individually. All approved.
**Outcome:** 4 headcounts freed; unified Web Team; single point of failure eliminated.
**Evidence:** 2023 Appraisal (Team Merger & Team Rename section).
**Wikilink:** [[2023-01-uiux-team-merger]]

---

### 2.3 Brought Blockchain Team into Frontend Sharing (2022)

**Outcome:** Internal programme expanded organically to another department without any formal mandate.

## Category 3: Influencing Upward (Senior Stakeholders)

## Category 5: HR and Organisational Feedback (Courage)

### 5.1 Public HR Transparency Feedback (2025)
**Stakeholder:** HR Team
**Context:** Company retreat was cancelled with 5 weeks of silence before any official communication, creating significant rumours and uneasiness.
**Influence:** Rather than informal complaints, Ronald drafted specific, constructive feedback in the annual appraisal — including a word-for-word example of what an appropriate interim message would have looked like.
**Outcome:** Feedback formally recorded; demonstrates willingness to speak truth to power in a professional, constructive manner.
**Evidence:** 2025 Appraisal (Suggestion/Feedback section — HR transparency paragraph).
