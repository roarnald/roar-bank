## Direct 1:1 Mentoring

### Jacky — NodeJS Full-Stack Development (2025)
**Context:** Jacky had never built a production server-side application. Ronald could have built the Lotto6D microservice himself (the faster, lower-risk path).

**What Ronald did:**
- Made a deliberate choice to appoint Jacky as primary developer rather than doing it himself — explicitly to extend team capability.
- Broke down deliverables into bite-sized, sequenced actionable steps.
- Provided working code samples from the New138 fullstack application as reference.
- Set up the delivery process (scaffolding, deployment procedures) to reduce unknowns.
- Remained available for blockers and collaborated to resolve them.
- Crucially: *never took over*. Maintained Jacky's ownership throughout.

**Outcome:**
- Production NodeJS microservice delivered by Jacky independently.
- Overseas team dependency for Lotto6D lifted.
- Jacky voluntarily presented all learnings at Frontend Sharing and publicly credited Ronald.
- Full-stack capability extended to a second engineer in the team.

**Source:** 2025 Appraisal; [[2025-01-lotto6d-nodejs-mentoring]]

### UIUX Team Developers — React/Web Transition (2023)
**Context:** 4 developers with a legacy HTML/CSS/JS background were merged into the React/Web Team following the UIUX dissolution. They were visibly anxious and reserved at the start of the year.

**What Ronald did:**
- Conducted individual consultations with each developer to understand their concerns.
- Paired each developer with a seasoned React engineer for direct support.
- Reassured the team: *"no questions are bad questions."*
- Drew on an army leadership analogy: *"In order to mold you into something great, we have to break you into as many pieces as possible"* — framing high standards not as punishment but as investment.
- Checked in regularly on progress and emotional comfort through the transition.

**Outcome:**
- All 4 developers successfully transitioned into the Web Team.
- By year-end, the team was operating as a single unified unit without the previous dependency on a legacy lead.
- Joanne (2023): *"Ronald has made a huge contribution in bringing up the competency of the Frontend team, equipping the team with the essential tools and knowledge to excel in their work."*

**Source:** 2023 Appraisal (Communication & Interpersonal, Coaching sections); [[2023-01-uiux-team-merger]]

### Junior Developer — Low-Level JavaScript Optimisation (2021)
**Context:** During a code review, Ronald spotted a junior developer using an inefficient JavaScript function.

**What Ronald did:**
- Did not simply provide a corrected version. Instead, taught the developer the conceptual difference between higher-level JS functions and lower-level for-loops.
- Drew on his own background in Assembly and C to explain why and when the lower-level approach is more efficient.
- Turned the code review into a teachable moment about performance trade-offs and the value of a low-level programming mental model.

**Outcome:**
- Junior developer gained understanding of performance thinking, not just a fix.
- The interaction became a model for how senior developers should approach code reviews: teach the principle, not the answer.
- Tsai Yu (appraiser): *"在指導junior時也能與他們一起研究討論，這讓團隊充滿了樂於學習的文化"* (When guiding juniors, researching and discussing together — this fills the team with a love of learning culture).

**Source:** 2021 Appraisal (Creativity & Problem Solving section)

## Group Mentoring and Facilitation

### Company-Wide Speaker Coaching (2023)
**Context:** Frontend developers were selected to present at a company-wide Architecture Sharing session — a high-visibility audience of non-technical and technical stakeholders.

**What Ronald did:**
- Organised and hosted multiple practice sessions before the main event.
- Gave structured feedback on delivery proficiency — tone, pacing, audience engagement.
- Coached speakers to move away from reading off technical slide text toward drawing relatable analogies for a mixed audience.

**Outcome:**
- Observable transformation in presenters: Ronald reported *"huge growth in their presentation skills. From reading off a slide of text and technical jargons, to drawing funny and relatable analogies to keep the audience engaged."*
- Several developers gained lasting confidence in public speaking, visible in subsequent team events.

**Source:** 2023 Appraisal (Non-Technical Achievements section)

### Frontend Sharing — 4-Year Knowledge Programme (2022–2025)
**Context:** Ronald identified that senior knowledge was not being systematically shared across the team. Individual developers accumulated expertise in silos.

**What Ronald did:**
- Re-initiated the Frontend Sharing programme in 2022 with 13+ sessions in year one.
- Personally hosted sessions on: Code Reusability, Recursive Rendering, Next.js, Shell & Terminal, Unit Testing.
- Recruited external speakers from Backend, DevOps, Design, and PM teams.
- Archived session notes on Notion for asynchronous access.
- In 2023: expanded to company-wide format; coached speakers individually before presenting.
- In 2024: delegated programme organisation entirely to senior developers as IC roles.
- In 2025: Jazz and Jane led independently; extended to Aquarix (external company) as participants and speakers.

**Outcome:**
- Programme ran continuously for 4+ years, self-sustaining by year 3.
- Multiple developers grew from no public speaking experience to presenting at company level.
- Blockchain team (separate department) requested to sit in — organic cross-team reach.

**Source:** 2022, 2023, 2024, 2025 Appraisals; [[2022-01-frontend-sharing-program]]

### Code Review Culture — Systemic Mentoring Infrastructure (2020+)
**Context:** Ronald joined during a period where code reviews were being introduced. He became one of the strongest advocates and practitioners.

**What Ronald did:**
- Advocated for code review standards and helped set them with senior developers.
- Consistently reviewed junior and mid-level Merge Requests with teaching intent — asking leading questions rather than providing direct answers (unless marked urgent).
- When tension arose from this approach, designed the Urgency Tagging system to protect the learning culture while accommodating genuine urgency. [[2021-11-urgency-tagging-workflow]]
- Encouraged junior and mid-level developers to become reviewers themselves — not just recipients.
- By 2022: *"Although code reviewing is still ran by the same few prominent seniors, we are slowly seeing new names that have come up as reviewers and I am immensely proud of that."*

**Outcome:**
- New reviewers emerged from the team.
- Code quality standards across the team rose measurably.
- Tsai Yu (2021): *"在指引年輕開發人員的同時增進自我能力"* (Guiding young developers while simultaneously improving your own abilities).

**Source:** 2021, 2022 Appraisals

### IC Role System — Leadership Mentoring at Scale (2024–2025)
**Context:** Ronald wanted to give every senior engineer a structured leadership experience without overwhelming them.

**What Ronald did:**
- Created a formal IC (In-Charge) role framework: each senior was assigned a main IC role for a team event.
- Each IC was responsible for: choosing their own Junior 2IC, planning, hosting, gathering feedback, and facilitating team discussions.
- Gave ICs full space to develop their own leadership style — no micromanagement.
- Was available to guide when needed but never overshadowed.

**Outcome:**
- Measurable confidence growth: *"The stutter from their voices, the redness on their faces and the mumbling of their words eventually fade away."* (Ronald, 2025 Appraisal)
- Events became self-sustaining — the team self-initiated social activities (Muay Thai, Pickleball, USS Halloween) without prompting.
- Joanne: *"I really have to give Ronald very big credit. He came up with a plan that lets the seniors grow and adapt in baby steps, not forced and not rushed... honestly I'm amazed by the individual improvements that came out of it."*

**Source:** 2025 Appraisal; [[2024-01-leadership-decentralisation]]
