# Leading Without Authority Examples

*Situations where Ronald influenced outcomes, drove change, or shaped decisions without formal authority over the people or outcomes involved.*

---

## Definition Used
Leading without authority = achieving meaningful outcomes by influencing people who do not report to you, changing decisions you did not have the power to mandate, or driving initiatives that required buy-in rather than directive.

---

## 1. Reversed PM Direction — Coin Backoffice Rewrite (2021)
**Role at the time:** Senior Software Engineer (not a lead)
**Who Ronald influenced:** Product Manager
**Authority level:** None — the PM owned the delivery decision

**What happened:** The PM directed a minimal fork of the legacy CASH repository for speed. Ronald assessed the long-term risk independently, concluded a full rewrite was necessary, communicated this clearly and compellingly, and proceeded with the rewrite.

**How he did it:**
- Demonstrated deep understanding of the codebase and its technical debt.
- Made a proactive, specific case — not a complaint, but a recommendation with rationale.
- Took full accountability for the outcome.

**Result:** The rewrite succeeded. Coin Backoffice became the benchmark quality frontend codebase. The decision was vindicated by results, not by authority.

**Source:** 2021 Appraisal; [[2021-01-coin-backoffice-rewrite]]

## 2. Dissolved the UIUX Team Without Authority Over Them (2023)
**Role at the time:** Lead Software Engineer (Web/React Team)
**Who Ronald influenced:** UIUX Team (4 developers + their lead), Frontend Internal Management
**Authority level:** None over UIUX Team; indirect influence over management

**What happened:** Ronald identified that the UIUX Team was a structural liability. He built the case, secured management buy-in, and then individually won the consent of every UIUX developer — promising that if even one person objected, he would design a custom arrangement for them.

**How he did it:**
- Built a business case grounded in market dynamics (modern developers know React, not legacy vanilla stacks).
- Presented data-backed reasoning to management rather than lobbying based on frustration.
- Respected the autonomy of UIUX team members — gave each a genuine voice and a genuine guarantee.
- Personally did the remediation work across 10+ UIUX repositories rather than just directing others to fix it.

**Result:** Unanimous approval. 4 headcounts freed. Single point of failure eliminated. Joanne: *"Ronald is always seeking for breakthrough in Frontend daily operation."*

**Source:** 2023 Appraisal; [[2023-01-uiux-team-merger]]

## 4. Established Architecture Review as a Mandatory Process (2023–2024)
**Role at the time:** Lead Software Engineer → Software Architect
**Who Ronald influenced:** PMs, Frontend Leads, developers
**Authority level:** Partial — could recommend, not mandate as Lead; gained authority as SA but still required PM buy-in

**What happened:** Ronald proposed an Architecture Review gate in the 2023 appraisal feedback. When he became Software Architect in 2024, he worked with PMs to make it part of the project initiation process — not by directive, but by demonstrating value on the first three reviews (Rollball, PNL, credit draw).

**How he did it:**
- Proposed formally in writing (appraisal suggestion section) rather than just raising verbally.
- Volunteered to do the work himself to prove the concept rather than asking for a process change upfront.
- Results on Rollball and PNL demonstrated value — the process was adopted because it worked.

**Result:** SA Review became standard for new frontend projects.

**Source:** 2023–2024 Appraisals; [[2024-01-sa-review-process]]

## 7. Influenced Security Culture Without Security Authority (2025)
**Role at the time:** Software Architect (not a Security role)
**Who Ronald influenced:** Development team, Security Team, Head of Architecture
**Authority level:** None over security decisions; brought in as advisor

**What happened:** When the Security Team pushed for dependency patch fixes, Ronald reframed the entire approach — from reactive one-by-one patching to automated workflow integration. He also self-initiated an OWASP security checklist, set up the Dependency Bot, and authored 3 security architecture proposals.

**How he did it:**
- Identified the systemic problem (patch cycle is unsustainable) and proposed a structural fix without being asked.
- Did the implementation work himself (Dependency Bot) to prove the concept.
- Proposed formal solutions (Digital Signature, Bot Prevention) to the security domain of a system he was not the original architect of.

**Result:** Security patch effort reduced 90%; proposals approved; OWASP checklist caught ~10 vulnerabilities before external pen test.

**Source:** 2025 Appraisal; [[2025-01-dependency-bot-90pct-reduction]], [[2025-10-security-checklist-owasp]]
