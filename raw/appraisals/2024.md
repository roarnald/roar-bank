| I am immensely grateful for the opportunity to step into this new role and discover what value I could bring to the team, while empowering others along the way. These are the key contributions and achievements of 2024: New workflow for New Frontend Projects (SA Review, Debugging of project level issues) Full Stack Application NodeJS Server Template Foundation Emergency Resource Code Push Server Setup Action Plan Frontend Leadership Decentralisation **New workflow for New Frontend Projects (SA Review, Debugging of project level issues)** With a new Architect role, we as a Team decided to implement my suggestion from 2023, to have an Software Architecture (SA) Review for new projects, just like how code reviews are for Change Requests (CRs). We managed to conduct SA Reviews for Rollball, PNL, and credit draw results and I found it to be very beneficial for helping steer some decisions and guide our developers in project setup. Additionally, these provided fresher challenges for our seniors and newer requirements apart from the daily CRs. I had also worked closely with our PMs and devs to help debug project level issues, and here are 2 examples: Rollball Project Sluggishness Initially when starting rollball, the user experience and overall responsiveness felt sluggish and it was challenging for PMs to pinpoint the issue and highlight to our developers. My role allowed the PMs to give a generic feel of the problem and what the goal was, so I could immediately start working towards an improvement.   Also, when the team was making use of Lottie files, they faced several challenges. I had to pleasure to work closely with the project lead and together, we managed to lay several facts in stone (e.g. High CPU usage, Vite issues with animation file imports, Lottie render methods, Lottie alternatives) which eventually led us to a solution of using an alternate asset type. 2\. Vite dynamic import issue (PNL & Rollball) As we transition away from webpack into vite for better Developer Experience, there were several in-depth issues within the tool that none of our Frontend developers, seniors, nor leads had a solution to. They still insisted on using vite due to its overwhelming faster build speeds, and fast development server. Fortunately, I had the time to dive in depth into these problems, provided solutions and guided the team to resolution. Today, these solutions have all been shipped: a. Vite dynamic import issue In technical terms, vite calculates its hash based on its contents, hence, there was a chance that the built hash for a page in Build\#5 was identical with Build\#6, and when clients cache these files, often, it would point to incorrect hashes, resulting in the page crashing. b. Vite Chunking issue Vite is a considerably new tool that took web development by storm, to a point where React recommends using vite when learning React. However, it was designed for small to medium applications as the only easy way to code split was to split third party libraries with local files. This will result in an unnecessary bloat in our library js chunk, affecting first load performance.  **Full Stack Application** In 2023, conversations about crafting a full stack Javascript application were floating around between the PMs and I. This year, we had a perfect problem to resolve using this new solution, which also aligns with the direction and vision of our CTO.  Looking back, this was the highlight of 2024\. From start to end, I worked alone as a solo full stack developer with some guidance from our previous Frontend lead, Tsai Yu, who was well versed in JS as a backend language, and several devops/solutions knowledge. Tsai Yu volunteered to be the temporary Fullstack/NodeJS Lead for us while we figured out our future plans.  In these months developing for New138导航网 as a full stack application, I have laid several foundations and wrote many best practices for future developers to follow, including but not exhaustive of are: Setting up Astro JS as a full stack service with dual mode to serve 2 requirements with the same codebase (Member, Admin) Dockerizing production full stack application for deployment Dockerizing infrastructure to allow easy running locally for developers Usage of redis to allow caching on server between pods Usage of Vault to store sensitive keys Writing of captcha service that was resilient to load testing Monitoring and load testing Writing of DB Script and making use of R-Deployment for full stack CRs Writing of Data migration DB Script from old product Setting up of DB Migration process This process has also enlightened me in several processes in web development and allowed me to further value add to my team. Together with Tsai Yu and the PM in-charge, Dominic, we managed to ship this application ahead of time and replaced the use of hundreds of elastic beanstalk instances with a single full stack application, significantly reducing cost and maintenance effort for the company. **NodeJS Server Template Foundation** As the dust settles for New138导航网, I took the opportunity on my own accord, to setup reference templates as for how a codebase of a Javascript Backend Server would look like. I added two variants, one with typescript and one without. I introduced several best practices like proper param validation, file-based routing, etc. This quickly yielded results as we were then asked to migrate a legacy application (WeChat Redirect) into Kubernetes. The templates allowed me to guide our developer to rewrite this legacy application and sped up the process of migrating the whole legacy service. **Emergency Resource** On top of doing research, I am glad that I was also able to help alleviate workload during time crunches, when PMs do not have enough resources. I was tasked to work alone on implementing translation into our CASH product, which ended up needing several changes in around 6 code bases. On the side, I also picked up a WISHLIST feature of refactoring our game rules repository architecture and has since shipped to production. **Code Push Server Template** As code push, a service that APP Team uses, is scheduled for deprecation, there was a request to find an open source alternative. For this issue, I have helped the APP Team to setup 2 working forked examples from legacy open source codebases and helped the team with dockerisation, testing and solution planning. We have also decided to keep a lookout  for the official standalone version (which has since been released as of today), and will be using that instead. **Action Plan Frontend** Action Plan is a group mainly composed of our company’s Architects, both Solution and Software, and CTO, driving innovation and discussion on new solutions. This was a group that was immensely helpful in raising concerns with our research efforts and helping tighten several details while I was working on the Full stack application. All research efforts were also properly documented on Google Drive, which includes things like: New Relic Data Pruning AI Code Assistant Research Bun.sh leverage Full stack application Node JS Backend Template Security Updates (Orion+ AWS Role Assuming, Vulnerabilities) Game Engine Exploration (Cocos) Code Push Server I had also allowed Joanne to rest assured knowing Frontend's effort and presentation will be handled properly during these discussions as she had to take time to rest up during her Maternity leave. **Leadership Decentralisation** As I stepped into this new role, my one motto for the year was to become the leader of our leaders. I wanted not only to be the lead for our Frontend Leads, but to empower our seniors to take on small leadership roles, to start instilling leadership qualities and grooming the future generations of leaders. I started by having an internal quota of making each senior take on at least 1 leadership position/task, and started assigning either routine tasks or event planning to them. For example: Frontend Sharing ICs Frontend Spotlight ICs Frontend Team Bonding Chalet ICs In addition, I allowed them to address the team directly without intervening, like opening the Frontend Sharing by introducing the speakers, which helped many of them gain confidence in their public speaking. This allowed the continuation of several internal team events that we crafted over the years, without solely relying on the Leads to initiate. Lastly, I am honoured and humbled to be approached by the Leads for solutions and advice. I am extremely grateful for their trust and will continue to push for growth alongside the team |
