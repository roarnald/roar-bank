| **Learning Ability & Improvement** |  |
| Appraisee Comment: This assessment year was a year of complex learning, especially so when tasks assigned to me were not merely a simple report, a change of styling or an addition of buttons. This year I am thankful for the guidance and opportunity to learn so much more on the more advanced and lesser touched aspects of development. A brief overview of what I am thankful for having experienced: Webpack setup of new repository Major Version upgrade of legacy repository Groovy scripting for Jenkins pipeline development New UI Library \- BlueprintJS & AntD 4.0 Frontend Optimisation \- Reduction of unnecessary renders, Efficiency of different state management, Repository File Structure Organisation, Mono-repository setup, Replacement of open source packages with from the ground written UI code Enlightening juniors on different thought process and debug process  While these are new experiences, the culture of constructive criticism and discussion have been a major factor in ensuring that we learn as a team and I am always learning from those around me, be it writing higher quality code, or sharing what I was taught.  Having said that, there has been occasions where the tension has been really high when junior developers have to go through vigorous code reviews even though the fix was urgent. As code reviewers tried their best to coach on the train of thought and methods to get to the solution, instead of plainly giving the better version of the code, sometimes, especially during urgent CRs, developers would feel immense pressure from wanting to push a fix quick while understanding the suggested concept. After two of such occasion, I took the initiative to talk to individual developers to understand and investigate the build up of such events and discussed with the Frontend Leads. I later found out that because there was a mismatch in emotions, where code reviewers were unable to correctly judge if the requester has an urgent task on hand, or not. Hence, they often try to help the requester learn, instead of blindly fix, by asking leading questions instead of providing an immediate fix.  These all led to the implementation of a new feature into our workflow: The Urgency Tagging. In order to resolve this mismatch in emotion, we allowed developers to label their Merge Requests (MR) with Urgent tags, if needed. (Or, if they prefer, to just mention that the MR is urgent) After a MR is tagged as Urgent, code reviewers are obliged to provide immediate solutions, and learning and be set aside for the time being.  This feature was really well received by the team, although it has only been used a couple of times, this showed the team that the leads are listening, and also gave them a peace of mind of having the option to label something as urgent. We also strongly encouraged not using the urgent tag, if time allows, to foster a positive learning culture. Although the feature was rarely used, during the follow-up feedback session that I conducted two months after implementation, most developers felt more at ease with the workflow in place. | 5 |
